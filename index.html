Pravesha (Beginner)

"Pravesha" involves the mastery of foundational techniques, postures, basic adavus (dance steps), and simple compositions. It marks the beginning of a dancer's formal training in Bharatnatyam and serves as a foundation upon which more advanced skills and repertoire are built.

Madhyama (Intermediate)

"Madhyama" typically refers to an intermediate level of proficiency or training.It marks a stage where dancers refine their skills, expand their repertoire, and begin to demonstrate a greater understanding of Bharatnatyam's nuances and intricacies.

Unnati (Advanced)

"Unnati" could be interpreted as the stage of progress or advancement in a dancer's training journey. It may represent a level beyond "Madhyama" (intermediate) where dancers continue to refine their skills, deepen their understanding of the art form, and expand their repertoire further.

Arangetram

"Arangetram" is a significant milestone and traditional debut performance in the journey of a Bharatnatyam dancer. It is a Tamil term that literally translates to "ascending the stage." The arangetram marks the culmination of years of rigorous training and preparation for a dancer.
